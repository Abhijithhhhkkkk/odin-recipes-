LASAGNA

Lasagna, also known by the plural form lasagne, is a type of pasta made in wide, flat sheets. It originates in Italian cuisine, where it is served in a number of ways , including in broth (lasagne in brodo), but is best known for its use in a baked dish made by stacking layers of pasta, alternating with fillings such as ragù (ground meats and tomato sauce), béchamel sauce, vegetables, cheeses

ingredients

  • Pasta: Lasagna noodles
  • Meat Sauce: Ground beef, Italian sausage, diced onion, minced garlic, crushed/sauce tomatoes, tomato paste, Italian seasoning, basil, oregano, salt, pepper, olive oil.
  • Cheese Filling: Ricotta or cottage cheese, shredded mozzarella, grated Parmesan, eggs, fresh parsley.
  • Veggies:
  • Flavor Boosters:A splash of wine, sugar to cut acidity, fennel seeds.
  • Binding: A beaten egg in the cheese mix.

Steps

  1. Cook Noodles: Boil if not using no-boil
  2. Sauce: Brown meat and veg, drain fat, add tomatoes, paste, seasonings, and simmer.
  3. Mix Cheese Filling: Combine ricotta, mozzarella, Parmesan, egg, parsley, salt in a bowl.
  4. Layer: Sauce, noodles, cheese mix, sauce, repeat in a baking dish
  5. Top & Bake: Finish with noodles, sauce, and extra cheese; bake until bubbly
